# Testosterone, Honesty, Formidability, and Cooperation Collapse

## Summary

Experimental evidence demonstrates that testosterone administration **reduces lying** in men. Combined with well-documented population-level declines in testosterone (~1% per year since the 1980s), this creates a plausible biological mechanism for increasing dishonesty at scale — degrading the trust signals that cooperative societies depend on.

A related body of research from evolutionary psychology shows that physical formidability (upper-body strength) predicts political attitudes toward redistribution and coalition formation. Weaker individuals preferentially support redistributive coalitions, while stronger individuals assert self-interest through competitive strategies. This formidability-politics link, mediated by the same testosterone-dependent pathways, suggests that population-level testosterone decline may shift political coalitional dynamics alongside honesty norms.

## Core Experimental Evidence

### Wibral et al. (2012) — "Testosterone Administration Reduces Lying in Men"

- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled. 91 healthy men (mean age ~24). 46 received 50mg transdermal testosterone (Testogel), 45 received placebo. Decisions made 21-24 hours post-administration.
- **Task:** Private die-roll with self-reported payoff. Participants could lie without detection to increase earnings.
- **Results:**
  - Testosterone group reported significantly lower payoffs (mean 3.33 vs. 4.18, p=0.005)
  - Reporting "5" (highest incentive to lie): 34.8% testosterone vs. 62.2% placebo (p=0.012)
  - Within-group: higher individual testosterone correlated with lower reported payoffs (r = -0.22, p=0.035)
- **Mechanism:** Authors propose testosterone enhances concern for self-image and pride, making "cheap" dishonesty feel degrading to one's status.
- **Source:** Wibral M, Dohmen T, Klingmuller D, Weber B, Falk A. Testosterone administration reduces lying in men. *PLoS ONE*. 2012;7(10):e46774.

### Nave et al. (2018) — Large-Scale Replication

- **Design:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled. 242 American men (mean age ~24). 125 received 100mg testosterone gel (Vogelxo), 118 received placebo.
- **Task:** Same die-roll paradigm as Wibral et al.
- **Results:**
  - Direction consistent with original: testosterone group reported lower payoffs (3.94 vs. 4.21)
  - Individual effect size: Cohen's d = -0.17 (non-significant alone)
  - **Meta-analytic pooling** with Wibral et al.: d = -0.27, 95% CI [-0.49, -0.06], p=0.01 (significant)
- **Conclusion:** "Suggestive but not conclusive" evidence for testosterone reducing dishonesty. Direction consistently replicated.
- **Source:** Nave G, Nadler A, Dubois D, Zava D, Camerer C, Plassmann H. Single-dose testosterone administration increases men's preference for status goods. *Scientific Reports*. 2018;8:12257.

### Kutlikova et al. (2023) — Strategic vs. Genuine Prosociality

- **Design:** 192 men, 150mg testosterone or placebo, reinforcement learning task performed privately or while observed.
- **Key Finding:** Testosterone **eliminated strategic (feigned) prosociality** — behavior where people act generous only when watched. Testosterone promoted honest, consistent behavior regardless of audience.
- **Interpretation:** Testosterone boosts "honest forms of status-seeking" rather than deceptive social signaling. Subjects on testosterone didn't perform for the audience — they behaved the same whether observed or not.
- **Source:** Kutlikova HH, et al. Testosterone eliminates strategic prosocial behavior through impacting choice consistency in healthy males. *Neuropsychopharmacology*. 2023;48:1719-1728.

### Dreher et al. (2016) — Prosocial and Antisocial Status-Enhancement

- **Design:** Double-blind, randomized. Ultimatum Game with punishment/reward options.
- **Finding:** Testosterone increased both punishment of unfair offers AND reward of generous offers. It enhanced status-maintenance through both positive and negative channels.
- **Interpretation:** Contradicts simple "testosterone = aggression" narrative. Testosterone promotes enforcement of social norms — punishing defectors and rewarding cooperators — which is precisely the behavior cooperative institutions require.
- **Source:** Dreher JC, et al. Testosterone causes both prosocial and antisocial status-enhancing behaviors in human males. *PNAS*. 2016;113(41):11633-11638.

## The Dual-Hormone Complication

The relationship between hormones and dishonesty is modulated by cortisol:

- **Mehta & Josephs (2010)** proposed that testosterone's effects on status-relevant behavior only manifest when cortisol is low. High cortisol blocks testosterone's status-seeking influence.
- **Harvard/UT Austin study:** Found that individuals with *both* elevated testosterone and cortisol were more likely to cheat — testosterone reducing fear of punishment while cortisol providing a stress-relief motive for cheating.
- **Meta-analytic evidence:** The interaction effect is significant but very small (r = -0.061, p=0.026, k=33 studies, N=8,538). Published studies average only 16% power to detect effects.
- **Implication for the declining-T thesis:** If chronic stress (elevated cortisol) is also rising alongside declining testosterone, the dual-hormone framework suggests an environment where testosterone's honesty-promoting effects are doubly suppressed — less testosterone overall, and what remains is blocked by cortisol.

## Population-Level Testosterone Decline

### The Secular Trend

- **Travison et al. (2007):** Age-independent decline in total testosterone estimated at ~1% per year. A 60-year-old measured in 1989 had higher testosterone than a different 60-year-old measured in 1995. Cross-sectional decline was 0.4%/year; longitudinal within-person decline was 1.6%/year.
- **Finnish cohort data:** Men aged 60-69 showed testosterone dropping from 21.9 nmol/L (born 1913-1922) to 13.8 nmol/L (born 1942-1951) — a 37% decline across birth cohorts.
- **Young men affected too:** Lokeshwar et al. found total testosterone significantly lower in 2011-2016 vs. 1999-2000 NHANES cycles in adolescent and young adult males (p<0.001).
- **Not explained by obesity alone:** Age-matched decline persists after adjusting for BMI, smoking, alcohol, and other confounders.

### Proposed Causes

- Endocrine-disrupting chemicals (plastics, pesticides, industrial chemicals)
- Sedentary lifestyles and obesity (partial, not sufficient explanation)
- Chronic stress and sleep disruption
- Dietary changes
- Environmental toxin accumulation

## Connection to Cooperation Collapse

The testosterone-honesty link provides a biological substrate for one mechanism of cooperation collapse:

1. **Testosterone promotes honest signaling.** Men with higher testosterone lie less, signal authentically (behave the same whether observed or not), and enforce social norms (punishing defectors, rewarding cooperators).

2. **Population testosterone is declining.** This is empirically established, age-independent, and not fully explained by lifestyle factors.

3. **Declining testosterone may degrade trust infrastructure.** If testosterone biologically supports honest behavior and norm enforcement, its secular decline removes a physiological foundation for the trust that cooperative societies require.

4. **Chronic stress compounds the problem.** Rising cortisol levels (from economic precarity, institutional dysfunction, information overload) may further suppress testosterone's prosocial effects via the dual-hormone mechanism.

5. **This creates a feedback loop:** Declining cooperation quality increases environmental stress, which further suppresses testosterone and honest signaling, which further degrades cooperation — a vicious cycle consistent with the signaling substrate degradation described in the whitepaper's diagnosis.

This is not a monocausal explanation. It is one biological channel among many (institutional, economic, informational) through which the cooperation radius contracts. But it is a channel with experimental evidence, population-level trend data, and a plausible evolutionary mechanism (status through honor rather than deception).

## Formidability, Coalitional Psychology, and Political Orientation

### The Ancestral Logic of Politics (Petersen, Sznycer, Sell, Cosmides, & Tooby, 2013)

Upper-body strength — a proxy for ancestral fighting ability — predicts men's political attitudes toward economic redistribution, modulated by socioeconomic status:

- **Design:** Data collected on bicep size, SES, and redistribution attitudes across US, Argentina, and Denmark.
- **Key Finding:** Stronger men more actively assert economic self-interest. Wealthy strong men oppose redistribution; low-SES strong men support it. Physically weak males are "more reluctant to assert their self-interest."
- **Gender specificity:** No link between strength and redistribution attitudes in women — consistent with sexual dimorphism in ancestral physical competition payoffs.
- **Implication:** Modern political decision-making is shaped by an evolved psychology designed for small-scale groups where physical formidability determined bargaining power. "The human mind is ecologically rational and designed for small-scale societies rather than means-end rational."
- **Source:** Petersen MB, Sznycer D, Sell A, Cosmides L, Tooby J. The ancestral logic of politics: upper-body strength regulates men's assertion of self-interest over economic redistribution. *Psychological Science*. 2013;24(7):1098-1103.

### Formidability and the Logic of Human Anger (Sell, Tooby, & Cosmides, 2009)

- The recalibrational theory of anger proposes that anger is a neurocognitive program that uses bargaining tactics (conditionally inflicting costs or withholding benefits) to resolve conflicts of interest.
- Individuals with greater fighting ability or capacity to confer benefits have a better bargaining position, are more prone to anger, prevail more in conflicts, and feel entitled to better treatment.
- This maps directly to "resource holding potential" (RHP) in evolutionary biology — the organism's ability to win conflicts determines its optimal strategy (fight vs. concede).
- **Source:** Sell A, Tooby J, Cosmides L. Formidability and the logic of human anger. *PNAS*. 2009;106(35):15073-15078.

### Physical Strength as a Heuristic Cue (Brown et al., 2023)

- Physically strong men are perceived as more conservative. Weak men do not signal a particular political affiliation.
- Formidability serves as a heuristic for coalitional alignment — strength signals endorsement of competitive social rules.
- **Source:** Brown M, et al. Physical strength as a heuristic cue of political conservatism. *Personality and Individual Differences*. 2023.

### Left-Wing Authoritarianism and Aversion to Formidable Leaders (Brown & Sacco, 2025)

- Higher left-wing authoritarianism (LWA) predicted aversion to physically strong men in leadership positions.
- Conservative perceivers preferred physically strong leaders, especially among conservative men.
- **Interpretation:** Those who benefit from hierarchies that reward physical formidability prefer formidable leaders; those who benefit from hierarchies that suppress physical formidability prefer weak leaders. Both are coalitional strategies optimizing for self-interest.
- **Source:** Brown M, Sacco DF. Heightened left-wing authoritarianism is associated with greater aversion to physical strength in male status allocation. *Personality and Individual Differences*. 2025.

### The Coalitional Logic: Weakness → Redistribution Preference

The evolutionary logic is straightforward:

1. **Ancestral environment:** Physical strength determined bargaining power. Strong individuals could acquire and defend resources through direct competition. Weak individuals could not.
2. **Optimal strategy for the weak:** Form coalitions that redistribute resources from the strong to the group. Support norms that suppress physical competition as a status-allocation mechanism. Prefer leaders who won't use formidability advantages.
3. **Optimal strategy for the strong:** Resist redistribution (if wealthy), enforce competitive norms, prefer leaders who signal formidability.
4. **Modern expression:** These evolved intuitions map onto left-right political attitudes toward economic redistribution, even though physical strength is irrelevant to modern economic competition.

### Health, Testosterone, and Coalitional Alignment

Connecting this to the testosterone-honesty findings:

- **Declining testosterone → declining formidability.** Population-level testosterone decline implies population-level decline in muscle mass, strength, and self-perceived fighting ability.
- **Declining formidability → shift in optimal coalitional strategy.** As men become weaker on average, the evolutionary calculus shifts: fewer men benefit from competitive norms, more men benefit from redistributive coalitions.
- **This is not a moral judgment.** Both strategies are rational responses to one's actual position. A weak individual *should* prefer redistribution — that's the strategy that maximizes their welfare. The concern is what happens when the shift is driven not by natural variation but by environmental degradation (endocrine disruptors, chronic stress, sedentary lifestyles).
- **The feedback loop compounds:** Weaker individuals form redistributive coalitions → redistributive norms reduce competitive pressure → reduced competitive pressure further reduces selection for formidability → population becomes weaker → stronger preference for redistribution. Combined with the testosterone-honesty link, this creates simultaneous shifts in both political orientation and honesty norms.

## Open Questions

- **Female populations:** The testosterone-honesty link has been studied primarily in men. Results in women are inconsistent (Kopsida et al., 2024 found no consistent hormone-unethicality relation in N=257 women).
- **Effect sizes:** Individual study effects are small (d ~ -0.17 to -0.27). Population-level consequences of small individual effects remain speculative.
- **Causal direction:** Does declining testosterone cause dishonesty, or does institutional dishonesty create the stress environment that suppresses testosterone? Likely bidirectional.
- **Intervention implications:** If testosterone is causally related to honest signaling, does this have policy implications? Environmental toxin regulation? Lifestyle interventions? This is politically fraught territory.
- **Formidability in modern contexts:** Physical strength is irrelevant to modern economic competition — yet it still predicts political attitudes. This suggests the psychology is calibrated to ancestral, not modern, environments. The mismatch may itself be a source of dysfunction.
- **Coalitional dynamics:** If population-level testosterone decline shifts coalitional preferences toward redistribution, does this create institutional capture risk? Redistributive institutions require high trust to function — but declining testosterone simultaneously degrades the honesty norms that trust depends on.
